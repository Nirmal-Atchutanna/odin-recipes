Idly Recipe

Back to Home

Description

Idly is a soft, fluffy, and steamed South Indian breakfast delicacy made from a fermented rice and urad dal batter. It’s light, wholesome, and pairs beautifully with coconut chutney and hot sambar.

These pillowy rice cakes are a staple in every South Indian home — gentle on the stomach and full of tradition, steamed to perfection without a drop of oil.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups parboiled rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking and grinding
  • Oil or ghee for greasing idly molds

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse rice and urad dal separately. Soak rice and dal (with fenugreek seeds) in water for 4–6 hours.
  2. Grind urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter. Grind rice to a slightly coarse batter.
  3. Mix both batters together in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well.
  4. Cover and let it ferment overnight or 8–12 hours, until the batter rises and turns airy.
  5. Grease idly molds and pour the batter into each slot.
  6. Steam for 10–12 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Let cool for a minute, then remove idlies with a spoon.
  8. Serve hot with chutney, sambar, or podi (spiced lentil powder).
