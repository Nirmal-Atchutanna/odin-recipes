Onion Dosa Recipe

Back to Home

Description

Ravva Dosa is a thin, crisp, lacy dosa made instantly from semolina (ravva), rice flour, and spices. Unlike traditional dosas, there's no fermentation needed — just mix, pour, and enjoy.

Speckled with cumin, green chilies, ginger, and sometimes onions or coriander, Ravva Dosa is crunchy, flavorful, and best enjoyed hot off the pan with coconut chutney or spicy tomato dip. A quick fix that never fails to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/ravva)
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1–2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • A few curry leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2½ to 3 cups water (to make a thin batter)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Preparation Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, rice flour, and all-purpose flour.
  2. Add chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, cumin, coriander leaves, curry leaves, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Pour in water gradually to make a thin, watery batter. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes.
  4. Stir well before using, as the mixture settles at the bottom.
  5. Heat a dosa tawa or non-stick pan until very hot. Splash the batter from the outside toward the center — do not spread like regular dosa.
  6. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges and holes. Cook on medium-high heat until the edges lift and turn golden.
  7. Do not flip. Fold and serve hot with chutney or sambar.
