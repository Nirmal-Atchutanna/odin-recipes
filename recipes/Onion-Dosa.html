Onion Dosa

Back to Home

Description

Onion Dosa is a thin, crispy South Indian crepe topped generously with finely chopped onions and green chilies. It’s a flavorful twist on the classic dosa — perfect for breakfast, dinner, or any time cravings.

The sizzle of onions hitting hot batter is music in every dosa stall. Slightly sweet, slightly sharp — it’s an iconic street-side comfort food you can make at home.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dosa batter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • Fresh coriander leaves (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for frying

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat a dosa tawa or non-stick pan on medium heat.
  2. Pour a ladle of batter and spread it in a thin circle.
  3. Quickly sprinkle chopped onions, chilies, and coriander evenly over the dosa.
  4. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges and on top.
  5. Cook until the bottom turns crisp and golden. No need to flip.
  6. Fold and serve hot with coconut chutney or spicy tomato chutney.
