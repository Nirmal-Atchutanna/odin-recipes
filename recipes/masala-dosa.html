Masala Dosa

Back to Home

Description

Masala Dosa is a beloved South Indian dish known for its crisp golden exterior and flavorful spiced potato filling. It's traditionally served with coconut chutney and sambar, making it a complete and comforting meal.

Made from a fermented rice and lentil batter, the dosa is thin like a crepe, yet satisfying like a feast. Whether enjoyed as a street food snack or a hearty breakfast, it’s a timeless classic that balances crunch, spice, and soul.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dosa batter (fermented rice and urad dal)
  • 2 medium-sized boiled potatoes
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • A few fresh curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Chutney and sambar (for serving)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped green chilies, curry leaves, and sliced onions. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  3. Sprinkle turmeric powder, then add the boiled and mashed potatoes. Mix well and season with salt.
  4. Cook the masala for 2–3 minutes. Set aside — this is your dosa filling.
  5. Heat a flat non-stick tawa or cast-iron pan. Pour a ladle of dosa batter and spread it into a thin circle.
  6. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the bottom turns golden and crispy.
  7. Place a spoonful of potato masala in the center and fold the dosa over it.
  8. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
