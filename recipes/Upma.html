Upma

Description

Ravva Upma is a warm, savory South Indian dish made from semolina, tempered spices, and a hint of vegetables. It’s quick, wholesome, and a staple breakfast across the country.

Fluffy, fragrant, and filling — upma is one of those dishes that comforts the soul and satisfies the belly with minimal effort.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/ravva)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 1 tsp chana dal
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 8–10 curry leaves
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2½ cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (optional)
  • Roasted cashews (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a pan on medium heat until it turns aromatic. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  3. Add urad dal, chana dal, and sauté till golden.
  4. Add green chili, ginger, curry leaves, and chopped onion. Sauté until onions soften.
  5. Pour in water, add salt, and bring to a boil.
  6. Lower the flame and slowly add the roasted semolina, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  7. Cook for 3–5 minutes until water is absorbed and the upma is fluffy.
  8. Garnish with coriander and roasted cashews. Serve hot.
