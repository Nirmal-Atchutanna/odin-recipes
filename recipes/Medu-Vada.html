Medu Vada Recipe

Back to Home

Description

Medu Vada is a crispy, savory fritter made from ground urad dal. Deep-fried and shaped like doughnuts, these crunchy delights are a beloved breakfast and snack across South India.

Golden on the outside, soft inside, and perfect with coconut chutney or piping hot sambar — Medu Vada is a celebration of texture and taste.

Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1 tsp chopped ginger
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Black pepper or cumin seeds (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse and soak urad dal for 4–5 hours. Drain and grind to a thick, fluffy batter.
  2. Add salt, ginger, chilies, onions, curry leaves, and optional spices. Mix well.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan. Wet your hand, scoop some batter, and shape like a doughnut with a hole.
  4. Carefully slide into hot oil. Fry on medium heat until golden and crisp.
  5. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
